ABOUT ME

My Early Years in Rome

I was born in Rome, Italy, one of the most fascinating cities in the world. I spent the first five years of my life surrounded by history, art, and the sound of Italian streets. Rome is not just ancient monuments; it’s a living museum filled with passion and tradition. From the Colosseum to the Vatican, every corner tells a story that connects centuries of culture, architecture, and human creativity.

Growing up there taught me to appreciate beauty and diversity. Italians are known for their warmth, their love of food, and their joy in life — values that I still carry with me today.

Chișinău

When I was five, my family moved to Chișinău, the capital of Republic of Moldova, and I lived there until I was nineteen. It’s a city that feels peaceful and close to nature, with wide tree-lined streets and friendly people. Moldova might be small, but it has a warm heart and a deep cultural identity.

The Moldovan culture is a mix of Romanian, Russian, and local traditions — you can feel that blend in the music, the language, and especially the food. People love to gather around the table, share placinte (traditional pies), and talk for hours. That sense of connection and hospitality taught me a lot about kindness and appreciation for simple moments.

My Hobbies

Swimming

Swimming has always been more than just a sport for me — it’s a rhythm that connects body and mind. I practiced swimming for two years, from the age of fourteen to sixteen, and took part in several competitions, achieving my best result with a second-place finish. Each time I dive into the water, I feel a calm clarity, as if every stroke washes away the noise of the world. It taught me discipline, patience, and the peace that comes from persistence.

Baking

Another passion of mine is baking — something I’ve been doing since I was thirteen. I started by taking small pastry courses to learn how to make macarons and cakes, and later turned that hobby into a small business, selling homemade cakes to friends and family. Baking gives me the same sense of focus that swimming does: it’s a balance of precision and creativity, science and emotion. There’s something deeply satisfying in creating something with your own hands that brings joy to others.



Dancing

Dancing has been a part of my life for as long as I can remember. I started ballroom dancing when I was five years old and continued until I was sixteen. During those years, I participated in many competitions across Moldova, performing with my teammates and earning several first-place awards. Ballroom dancing taught me rhythm, confidence, and the beauty of expressing emotion through movement. It’s more than a sport — it’s a dialogue between discipline and creativity, where every step becomes a story told without words.
